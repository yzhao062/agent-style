## Methods

We represent each input graph \(G=(V,E,X)\) with a graph isomorphism network encoder that maps node attributes and topology to a graph embedding. Each encoder block applies an MLP update to the sum of a node representation and its neighbor representations, followed by batch normalization, ReLU activation, and dropout. We use five message-passing layers with 128 hidden units, dropout \(p=0.2\), and residual connections after the second layer to stabilize deeper aggregation. A sum readout pools node states from the final layer into a graph vector, and a two-layer projection head maps this vector to a 64-dimensional contrastive space. We train the encoder with Adam at a learning rate of \(10^{-3}\) and weight decay \(10^{-5}\).

For contrastive pretraining, we treat two stochastic augmentations of the same graph as a positive pair. Each augmentation independently drops 10% of nodes, perturbs 10% of edges, and masks 15% of node features, while preserving graph connectivity when possible. These operations force the encoder to retain graph-level structure rather than memorize local attributes. Graphs sampled from other training instances in the same minibatch serve as negatives. We optimize the normalized temperature-scaled cross-entropy loss over projected embeddings, where cosine similarity measures agreement between views. For an anchor view, the loss increases similarity to its paired view and decreases similarity to all other views in the batch. We use a batch size of 256, temperature \(\tau=0.2\), and train for 200 epochs with early stopping based on the moving average of the contrastive loss.

At inference, we discard the projection head and compute the normalized graph embedding \(h(G)\) from the encoder readout. The method assigns high anomaly scores to graphs whose embeddings have weak support under the training distribution. For each test graph, we compute cosine distances to all training graph embeddings and average the distances to the \(k=20\) nearest neighbors. The anomaly score is \(s(G)=1-\frac{1}{k}\sum_{i \in N_k(G)} \cos(h(G),h_i)\), where \(N_k(G)\) denotes the nearest training embeddings. We rank graphs by this score, with larger values indicating stronger deviation from the learned graph-level representation.
