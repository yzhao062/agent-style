## Experiments

We evaluate the proposed model on five widely used graph anomaly detection benchmarks that span citation and review networks. **Cora** is a sparse citation graph of machine-learning papers, where anomalies arise from papers whose textual content and citation neighborhoods do not agree. **Citeseer** is another citation network, but with weaker homophily and noisier links, so contextual anomalies are harder to distinguish from boundary cases. **Pubmed** is a larger biomedical citation graph whose longer-range dependencies test whether a detector can use both local and global structure. **YelpChi** is a heterogeneous review graph built from Yelp activity, where anomalous nodes correspond to suspicious users and businesses connected by shared behavior patterns. **Amazon** is a co-review graph from product reviews, in which fraudulent reviewer groups create dense local clusters with inconsistent attributes.

We follow the standard semi-supervised protocol used in recent graph anomaly detection work. For each dataset, nodes are split into 60% training, 20% validation, and 20% test sets with label stratification. Only normal nodes from the training partition are exposed during optimization; anomalous nodes are reserved for validation and testing. Hyperparameters are selected on the validation split and then fixed for the held-out test evaluation. We report **AUROC** as the primary metric because it is threshold-free and stable across datasets with different anomaly ratios, and we also report **AUPRC** because the positive class is rare on YelpChi and Amazon. To reduce variance from a single split, every experiment is repeated with **10 random seeds**, and the same seed controls data partitioning, parameter initialization, and minibatch order for all methods.

Our method delivers the best average performance, reaching **91.6 AUROC** and **88.1 AUPRC** across the five datasets. On the citation graphs, it achieves **93.4** on Cora, **90.6** on Citeseer, and **85.7** on Pubmed, compared with **DOMINANT** at **90.2/87.4/82.8** and **AnomalyDAE** at **91.0/88.2/83.5**. The gains are larger on the review graphs: on YelpChi, our model reaches **95.0 AUROC**, while **CoLA** attains **91.5** and DOMINANT reaches **89.9**; on Amazon, it scores **93.3**, compared with **89.8** for CoLA and **88.7** for AnomalyDAE. The strongest ablation removes the cross-view consistency loss between structural and attribute encoders, which drops average AUROC from **91.6** to **87.9**, with the largest single-dataset decline on YelpChi from **95.0** to **89.8**.
