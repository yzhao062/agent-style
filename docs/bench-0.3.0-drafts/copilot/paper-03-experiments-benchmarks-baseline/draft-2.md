## Experiments

We evaluate the proposed method on five widely used graph-anomaly-detection benchmarks that vary in scale, sparsity, and anomaly type. **Cora** is a citation network of machine-learning papers, where anomalies correspond to nodes whose attributes are inconsistent with their local citation neighborhoods. **Citeseer** is a sparser citation graph with high feature noise, making rare-topic nodes difficult to distinguish from genuine outliers. **Pubmed** is a larger biomedical citation network that tests whether anomaly scores remain stable on denser and more class-imbalanced graphs. **Amazon** is a co-purchase network in which anomalous products exhibit irregular metadata together with atypical connectivity patterns. **YelpChi** is a review-based graph where fraudulent behavior appears through both suspicious node features and abnormal relational structure.

For all datasets, we follow a standard unsupervised evaluation protocol. The model is trained without anomaly labels on the observed graph and node attributes, while benchmark labels are used only for validation and final testing. We randomly partition labeled nodes into **10% validation** and **90% test** sets, tune hyperparameters on the validation split, and then report performance on the held-out test split. Because anomaly detection is inherently imbalanced, we use **ROC-AUC** as the primary metric and **Average Precision (AP)** as a complementary metric that better reflects ranking quality under skewed class ratios. To reduce sensitivity to stochastic effects, each configuration is run with **10 random seeds** controlling initialization, sampling, and split generation, and we report mean ± standard deviation across seeds.

Our method achieves the best average performance across all five benchmarks, with an overall mean of **89.7 ROC-AUC** and **82.4 AP**. On **Cora**, it reaches **88.9 AUC**, improving over **DOMINANT** (**84.3**) and **AnomalyDAE** (**86.1**). On **Citeseer**, it obtains **87.2 AUC**, compared with **82.8** for DOMINANT and **84.6** for AnomalyDAE. The advantage persists on larger graphs: **91.3 AUC** on **Pubmed**, **88.1** on **Amazon**, and **93.0** on **YelpChi**, where the strongest competitor, **ComGA**, achieves **90.8**. The strongest ablation removes the **cross-view contrastive consistency** component, reducing average AUC from **89.7** to **85.6**; this drop is larger than removing adaptive neighborhood reweighting (**87.4**) or residual score fusion (**88.2**), indicating that cross-view alignment is the main driver of the observed gains.
