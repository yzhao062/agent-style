### Specific Aim 1: Concept-Drift-Adaptive Outlier Detection for High-Velocity Clinical Telemetry

**Research question.** Can a single online detector maintain calibrated false-alarm rates on continuously arriving multivariate clinical signals (ECG, SpO2, invasive pressures sampled at 125-500 Hz) when both the inlier distribution and the contamination rate drift across patient cohorts, care units, and device generations?

**Novelty over prior approaches.** Existing streaming detectors fall into two camps. Window-based methods such as LODA, xStream, and RRCF refit summary structures on a rolling buffer and assume stationary contamination. Deep autoencoder pipelines such as MemStream and USAD capture multivariate dependence but require offline retraining once drift exceeds a fixed tolerance. Neither class jointly adapts the score function and the decision threshold under verified drift. The proposed framework, DRIFT-OD, couples a density-ratio drift estimator to a conformal threshold updater, producing a detector whose per-window false-alarm rate is provably bounded under exchangeability within each detected drift segment. To our knowledge, no prior streaming detector offers a comparable finite-sample guarantee on non-stationary clinical data.

**Three-task plan.**
1. *Algorithm design (months 1-9).* Derive the conformal update rule under detected drift and prove a finite-sample bound on segment-conditional Type-I error. Milestone: empirical bound within 0.01 of nominal on synthetic drift mixtures and on the MIMIC-IV-WDB cohort.
2. *Scalable implementation (months 6-18).* Engineer an open Apache Flink reference release sustaining sub-100 ms per-sample latency at 1 kHz aggregate input. Milestone: PhysioNet benchmark showing AUROC parity with offline isolation forest while meeting the latency target.
3. *Prospective validation (months 15-30).* Deploy on de-identified ICU telemetry under existing IRB coverage at a partnering academic medical center. Milestone: recall of 0.80 against clinician-adjudicated artifacts at fewer than one false alarm per patient-hour across three care units.

**Intellectual-merit alignment.** *Scientific rigor* rests on the formal Type-I bound, a pre-registered evaluation protocol, and a fully reproducible open benchmark. *Investigator qualifications* draw on the PI's peer-reviewed contributions to streaming anomaly detection and the co-PI's funded clinical informatics research. *Feasibility* is supported by an in-place waveform archive, active IRB coverage, and a prototype already achieving sub-100 ms latency on a single GPU node, indicating that the engineering targets above are reachable within the proposed timeline.
