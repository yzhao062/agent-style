## Experiments

We evaluate our approach on five widely-used benchmarks spanning citation, e-commerce, review, and financial domains. Cora is a citation network in which injected structural and attribute anomalies serve as a controlled testbed. Amazon consists of co-purchase relations among products, with labeled fraudulent sellers identified through platform-level flags. YelpChi contains hotel and restaurant reviews, where spam reviewers form the anomaly class among genuine reviewers. T-Finance is a transaction graph in which accounts flagged for illicit activity such as money laundering are treated as anomalies. DGraph-Fin is a large-scale financial fraud benchmark with over three million nodes, providing a stress test for scalability under long-tailed anomaly distributions.

For each dataset, we use a 40/20/40 train/validation/test split stratified by class label, and we evaluate using AUC-ROC and AUPRC, with AUPRC serving as the primary metric given the extreme class imbalance common in anomaly detection. All results are averaged over five independent runs with seeds {0, 1, 2, 3, 4}, and we report mean ± standard deviation; early stopping is triggered by validation AUPRC with a patience of twenty epochs. Hyperparameters are selected via grid search on the validation split, holding the test split strictly unseen until final evaluation. Every baseline is re-run under the same split and seed regime using the authors' released code to ensure a controlled comparison.

Our method achieves an average AUPRC of 0.847 across the five benchmarks, outperforming the strongest reconstruction baseline, DOMINANT (0.762), by 8.5 points, and the recent contrastive baseline CoLA (0.801) by 4.6 points. The gains are most pronounced on DGraph-Fin (+6.9 AUPRC over CoLA) and YelpChi (+5.2 AUPRC), where heterophilic structure penalizes reconstruction-only objectives. In the ablation study, removing the dual-view structure-attribute contrastive objective produces the largest drop, reducing average AUPRC by 3.4 points to 0.813; disabling the adaptive neighborhood reweighting module costs a further 1.9 points, confirming that both components contribute complementary signal. Qualitative analysis of learned embeddings shows that anomalies form visibly tighter clusters under the full model than under either ablated variant.
