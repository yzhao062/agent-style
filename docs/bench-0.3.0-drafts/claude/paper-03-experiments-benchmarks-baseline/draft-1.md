## Experiments

We evaluate our method on five widely used graph anomaly detection benchmarks. YelpChi contains 45,954 hotel and restaurant reviews with a 14.5% fraction of filtered (anomalous) reviews, connected by reviewer, product, and time-window relations. Amazon consists of 11,944 musical-instrument reviewers linked by co-review and co-rating edges, with benign users labeled via helpful-vote ratios. T-Finance is a transaction graph of 39,357 user accounts where anomalies correspond to accounts flagged for fraud, money laundering, or bust-out activity. Reddit is a user–subreddit interaction graph with 10,984 nodes in which banned users serve as the anomaly class. DGraph-Fin is a large-scale financial social network of roughly 3.7M nodes, where overdue borrowers (about 1.3%) form a highly imbalanced anomaly set.

For each dataset we adopt a stratified 40/20/40 train/validation/test split, preserving the original anomaly ratio in every partition. Because the task is heavily class-imbalanced, we report AUC-ROC as the primary metric and AUC-PR as a secondary metric; macro-F1 is included in the appendix for completeness. All models are trained with Adam (lr = 5e-4, weight decay = 1e-5) for up to 300 epochs with early stopping on validation AUC-PR (patience = 30). Every result is averaged over five runs with random seeds {0, 1, 2, 3, 4} controlling split sampling, parameter initialization, and dropout masks; we report mean ± standard deviation and use a paired t-test (α = 0.05) against the strongest baseline.

Our model (GADiff) attains an average AUC-ROC of 91.7 ± 0.4 across the five benchmarks, improving over the best spectral baseline BWGNN (88.9 ± 0.6) by 2.8 points and over the reconstruction-based DOMINANT (82.3 ± 1.1) by 9.4 points. Gains are largest on T-Finance (+3.6) and DGraph-Fin (+3.1), where heterophilic neighborhoods penalize message-passing baselines. An ablation removing the learned frequency-adaptive filter reduces AUC-ROC to 89.2 ± 0.5 (−2.5), confirming that the filter, rather than the contrastive pretext alone, accounts for most of the improvement; disabling the anomaly-aware negative sampler yields a smaller drop of 1.1 points, while replacing our loss with vanilla binary cross-entropy loses 1.7 points on average.
