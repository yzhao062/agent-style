### Specific Aim 1: Dual-Timescale Adaptive Outlier Detection for Non-Stationary Clinical Data Streams

**Research question.** Can online outlier detectors maintain bounded false-alarm rates and high sensitivity to clinically meaningful anomalies under the non-stationary physiology, shifting care protocols, and device heterogeneity that characterize continuous clinical telemetry (ICU vitals, ECG, CGM)?

**Novelty over prior work.** Existing streaming detectors (e.g., HS-Trees, xStream, LODA, RRCF) assume stationary distributions or rely on periodic batch retraining, and clinical anomaly detectors typically use fixed thresholds or single-patient models that fail to generalize across units. We propose a *dual-timescale adaptive ensemble* that (i) decouples representation drift from decision-boundary drift via a two-level change-point monitor, (ii) performs memory-bounded model refresh with provable dynamic-regret guarantees under piecewise-stationary streams, and (iii) calibrates per-patient thresholds through conformal prediction for distribution-free coverage. No prior method unifies these three properties.

**Three-task plan with measurable milestones.**

- **Task 1.1 — Algorithmic foundations (Months 1–10).** Develop and analyze the dual-timescale ensemble and prove sublinear dynamic-regret bounds under piecewise-stationary drift. *Milestone:* theoretical results plus synthetic benchmarks showing ≥20% AUPRC improvement over HS-Trees and xStream across three canonical drift regimes.
- **Task 1.2 — Multi-cohort clinical validation (Months 8–20).** Evaluate on MIMIC-IV waveform, eICU-CRD, and a de-identified local ICU cohort under an existing IRB protocol. *Milestone:* AUROC ≥0.90 for clinician-adjudicated deterioration events at ≤3 false alarms per patient-day and <50 ms per-sample latency on commodity hardware.
- **Task 1.3 — Calibration and robustness (Months 16–24).** Integrate conformal thresholds and stress-test against device swaps and protocol changes. *Milestone:* marginal coverage within ±2% of the nominal level across five held-out sites; open-source reference implementation released under a permissive license.

**Alignment with NSF intellectual-merit criteria.** *Scientific rigor:* theoretical guarantees are paired with prospective multi-site evaluation using pre-registered endpoints and pre-committed statistical analyses. *Investigator qualifications:* the PI has authored peer-reviewed work on concept drift and online learning at NeurIPS, ICML, and KDD, and has led two prior federally funded clinical informatics projects; the co-PI directs the medical center's clinical data warehouse and contributed to MIMIC-IV curation. *Feasibility:* preliminary results (Supplementary Fig. S1) show a 12% AUPRC gain on a pilot eICU slice, IRB approvals for the local cohort are already in place, and all benchmark datasets, GPU resources, and engineering personnel are secured for the full project period.
