### Specific Aim 1: Adaptive, Concept-Drift-Aware Outlier Detection for Streaming Clinical Data

**Research question.** How can outlier-detection models for high-frequency clinical streams (ICU vital signs, continuous glucose monitors, wearable ECG) maintain bounded false-alarm rates and high sensitivity when patient physiology, sensor calibration, and population case mix drift on timescales from minutes to months?

**Novelty over prior approaches.** Existing streaming detectors (HS-Trees, RRCF, LODA variants) assume stationarity or uniform drift, and recent deep autoencoder methods require offline retraining windows incompatible with bedside latency budgets. Clinical early-warning systems (MEWS, NEWS2, eCART) rely on fixed thresholds that ignore individualized baselines. We propose a hierarchical, per-patient–per-cohort detector that couples (i) a lightweight online kernel density estimator for fast local drift, (ii) a change-point–gated cohort model that pools information across similar patients via a matrix-valued Pólya–Gamma prior, and (iii) a calibration layer that guarantees an operator-specified false-alarm budget under arbitrary drift using conformal martingale bounds. No prior work unifies per-patient adaptation, cohort-level pooling, and distribution-free error control in a single streaming detector.

**Three-task plan and milestones.**
- *Task 1.1 (Months 1–9): Algorithm design and theoretical analysis.* Derive regret and false-alarm bounds for the conformal martingale layer under piecewise-stationary drift. Milestone: a proven O(√T log T) regret bound and a public reference implementation.
- *Task 1.2 (Months 6–18): Retrospective benchmarking.* Evaluate on MIMIC-IV waveform, eICU-CRD, and the PhysioNet 2019 Sepsis Challenge. Milestone: ≥15% AUROC gain and ≥30% reduction in alarms-per-patient-day against HS-Trees, RRCF, and a transformer-autoencoder baseline at matched sensitivity.
- *Task 1.3 (Months 12–24): Prospective silent deployment.* Integrate an HL7/FHIR adapter at a partner ICU. Milestone: 90-day silent run on ≥200 admissions with detector latency <250 ms per sample and clinician-adjudicated precision ≥0.6 on flagged events.

**Intellectual-merit alignment.** *Scientific rigor:* pre-registered evaluation protocol, formal error-rate guarantees, and open code and data splits. *Qualifications of investigators:* the PI has seven years of streaming-analytics publications at KDD and NeurIPS; the co-I is a board-certified intensivist who provides clinical adjudication and IRB-approved data access. *Feasibility:* preliminary work on a 12,000-patient MIMIC-IV subset already yields a 22% alarm-rate reduction at matched recall, and the FHIR adapter operates in a sandbox environment at the partner site.
