## Experiments

We evaluate on five benchmark graphs that span citation, e-commerce, and financial domains. Cora and CiteSeer are citation networks of 2,708 and 3,327 nodes in which we inject structural and attribute anomalies using the standard protocol from prior graph anomaly work. YelpChi contains 45,954 hotel and restaurant reviews labeled as spam or genuine, with reviewer-to-product edges that capture collusion patterns. Amazon includes 11,944 users of musical-instrument reviews, where reviewers with similar rating histories share an edge and fraudsters form dense subgraphs. T-Finance, the largest benchmark in our suite, has 39,357 accounts and 21 million edges, with 4.6% of nodes flagged by the data provider as money-laundering agents.

For every dataset we sample 40% of nodes for training, 20% for validation, and the remaining 40% for testing, stratified by label so that each split preserves the anomaly ratio. We select the checkpoint that maximizes AUC-ROC on the validation split, and we report both AUC-ROC and AUPRC on the held-out test split, since AUPRC reflects performance under the heavy class imbalance typical of fraud graphs more faithfully than AUC-ROC alone. Each experiment repeats with five random seeds (0 through 4), and we report the mean and standard deviation across runs. We tune hyperparameters once on Cora and reuse them across the other four datasets to limit per-dataset tuning leakage.

Our method, GADiff, reaches a mean test AUC of 94.8% on Cora, 93.1% on CiteSeer, 89.7% on YelpChi, 91.2% on Amazon, and 88.4% on T-Finance. DOMINANT, the reconstruction-based baseline most often cited in prior work, trails by 5.8 points on average, with its largest gap of 8.3 points on YelpChi, where attribute reconstruction alone fails to separate colluding reviewers. CoLA, a contrastive method, is the strongest external baseline and stays within 2.1 points of GADiff on the citation graphs but falls behind by 4.9 points on T-Finance, where long-range propagation matters more than local contrast. The strongest single component in our design is the heterophily-aware diffusion kernel: replacing it with a standard symmetric Laplacian drops test AUC by 3.4 points averaged across the five datasets, and by 5.7 points on T-Finance alone. Removing the anomaly-aware negative sampler costs 1.8 points on average, and removing the auxiliary reconstruction loss costs 0.9 points.
