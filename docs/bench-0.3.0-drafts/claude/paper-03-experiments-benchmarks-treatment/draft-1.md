## Experiments

We evaluate on five public benchmarks that together span citation networks, review platforms, and financial graphs. Cora contains 2,708 papers linked by 5,429 citations, with 150 anomalous nodes injected through structural and attribute perturbation. Amazon consists of 11,944 product-review accounts where 821 fraudulent reviewers carry platform-moderator flags. YelpChi includes 45,954 hotel and restaurant reviews, 14.5 percent of which the platform filter marks as deceptive. T-Finance captures 39,357 anonymized account nodes linked by transaction edges, with 1,803 nodes tagged as money-laundering suspects. Reddit supplies a temporal graph of 10,984 user-subreddit interactions, in which 366 banned users form the anomaly class.

We split each graph into 40 percent training, 20 percent validation, and 40 percent test nodes using stratified sampling, so the positive rate stays constant across folds. Our metrics are ROC-AUC and PR-AUC, chosen because label imbalance ranges from 3 to 15 percent and threshold-free scores avoid arbitrary cutoffs. Every configuration runs under ten random seeds (0 through 9) that govern weight initialization, dropout masks, and the stratified split itself; we report mean and standard deviation across the ten runs. Hyperparameters are tuned on the validation ROC-AUC of seed 0 and then frozen for the remaining nine, so no test-set leakage inflates the reported numbers.

Our method, ScaleGAD, reaches an average ROC-AUC of 91.8 across the five benchmarks, a 3.9-point gain over DOMINANT (87.9) and a 2.6-point gain over the stronger contrastive baseline CoLA (89.2). The improvement is largest on the two fraud graphs: YelpChi climbs from 79.4 under DOMINANT to 86.1, and T-Finance climbs from 88.3 to 93.7. PR-AUC follows the same ordering, with ScaleGAD at 68.4, CoLA at 64.1, and DOMINANT at 60.2. The strongest ablation removes the multi-scale spectral filter and replaces it with a fixed low-pass filter; this variant drops to 87.4 ROC-AUC, a 4.4-point loss that exceeds the gap to either baseline and concentrates on graphs whose anomalies sit in the high-frequency band.
