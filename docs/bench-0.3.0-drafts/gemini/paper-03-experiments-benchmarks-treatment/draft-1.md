## Experiments

We evaluate our model on five diverse benchmark datasets for graph anomaly detection to ensure robust performance across different domains. The Cora dataset consists of a citation network where researchers identify misclassified or adversarial publications as anomalous nodes. Amazon represents a co-purchase network where fraudulent user reviews and suspicious product links serve as the primary anomalies. YelpChi contains hotel and restaurant reviews where the task involves detecting spammer groups and individual fraudulent reviewers. Bitcoin-OTC provides a signed trust network of cryptocurrency traders where low-trust or malicious actors represent the target anomalies. The Elliptic dataset tracks Bitcoin transactions over time to distinguish between licit entities and illicit actors involved in money laundering schemes.

Our evaluation protocol prioritizes reproducibility and statistical rigor through standardized data processing. We adopt a consistent 70/10/20 split for training, validation, and testing across all five datasets to maintain comparability. To quantify performance, we use the Area Under the Receiver Operating Characteristic curve (ROC-AUC) and the Area Under the Precision-Recall curve (AUPRC). These metrics are essential because they effectively handle the extreme class imbalance typically found in anomaly detection scenarios. We execute each experiment using 10 distinct random seeds to initialize model parameters and shuffle data splits. Reporting the mean performance and standard deviation for every configuration accounts for stochastic variance during the training process.

The experimental results indicate that our proposed architecture consistently outperforms established baselines in detection accuracy. On the Elliptic dataset, our model achieves a ROC-AUC of 0.924 (±0.003). This result exceeds the 0.852 (±0.007) of the GCN-AE baseline and the 0.881 (±0.005) of the Dominant framework. We observe a similar trend on YelpChi, where our model reaches an AUPRC of 0.765, outperforming the closest baseline by 5.2%. In our ablation study, we assess the contribution of the structural attention mechanism by replacing it with a standard mean-pooling layer. This modification reduces the ROC-AUC on the Bitcoin-OTC dataset from 0.915 to 0.868. Such a drop indicates that the attention mechanism is the most critical component for identifying anomalous topological patterns. The model maintains this performance advantage across all tested graph densities.
