## Experiments

We evaluate our architecture on five standard graph anomaly detection benchmarks to demonstrate performance across distinct domains. Cora serves as a citation network where researchers identify misclassified or adversarial publications as anomalous nodes. Amazon represents a product co-purchase network where fraudulent reviews and suspicious item links constitute the primary anomalies. YelpChi consists of restaurant reviews where the task involves detecting spammer groups and malicious individual reviewers. Bitcoin-OTC provides a signed trust network of traders where low-trust actors represent the target anomalies. The Elliptic dataset tracks financial transactions to distinguish between licit entities and illicit actors involved in money laundering schemes.

The evaluation protocol prioritizes reproducibility through standardized data processing and strict metric selection. A consistent 70/10/20 split for training, validation, and testing across all datasets ensures comparability. We measure performance using the Area Under the Receiver Operating Characteristic curve (ROC-AUC) and the Area Under the Precision-Recall curve (AUPRC). These metrics effectively address the extreme class imbalance inherent in anomaly detection tasks. Each experiment runs across 10 distinct random seeds to account for parameter initialization and data shuffling variance. We report the mean performance and standard deviation to provide a statistically rigorous assessment of model stability.

Results show that our architecture consistently outperforms established baselines in detection accuracy. On the Elliptic dataset, the model achieves a ROC-AUC of 0.924 (±0.003), exceeding the 0.852 of GCN-AE and 0.881 of DOMINANT. Similar gains appear on YelpChi, where the model reaches an AUPRC of 0.765, representing a 5.2% improvement over the strongest competitor. In our ablation study, we replace the structural attention mechanism with a standard mean-pooling layer to isolate its contribution. This change reduces the ROC-AUC on Bitcoin-OTC from 0.915 to 0.868, identifying attention as the most critical component for capturing anomalous topological features. The model retains this competitive advantage regardless of the underlying graph density.
