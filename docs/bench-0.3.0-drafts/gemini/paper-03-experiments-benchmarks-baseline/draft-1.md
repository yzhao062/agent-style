## Experiments

We evaluate our proposed Graph-Anomaly-Detection Framework (GAD-Net) across five diverse benchmark datasets to demonstrate its robustness and generalizability. The Cora dataset is a citation network where nodes are publications and anomalies are injected through structural and attribute perturbations. Citeseer serves as another citation-based benchmark that tests the model's ability to identify irregular publication patterns within a sparse graph structure. The Amazon dataset consists of a large-scale product review graph where anomalies represent fraudulent user accounts and deceptive reviews. YelpChi is a restaurant review dataset focused on detecting spam content through the analysis of user-item interaction patterns. Finally, the Disney dataset is a real-world co-purchase graph where anomalies are defined as products belonging to rare or irregular category clusters.

For the evaluation protocol, we adopt a standard 70/10/20 split for training, validation, and testing across all datasets to ensure a consistent comparison. Performance is quantified using the Area Under the Receiver Operating Characteristic curve (ROC-AUC) and the Precision-Recall Area Under the Curve (PR-AUC), which are particularly effective for the class-imbalanced nature of anomaly detection tasks. To account for stochasticity in initialization and graph sampling, all experiments are executed across 10 independent trials using different random seeds, with mean values and standard deviations reported for all metrics. Hyperparameters were tuned via grid search on the validation set, specifically optimizing the learning rate and the weight of the reconstruction loss.

Our main results indicate that GAD-Net consistently outperforms state-of-the-art baselines, including DOMINANT and GCN-AE, across all five benchmarks. On the YelpChi dataset, GAD-Net achieved an ROC-AUC of 91.4%, representing a 4.2% improvement over DOMINANT (87.2%) and a 5.8% lead over GCN-AE (85.6%). Similarly, on the Amazon dataset, our framework reached 88.7% PR-AUC, significantly surpassing the 82.1% achieved by the nearest competitor. We also conducted an extensive ablation study to analyze the contribution of the Multi-Scale Attention (MSA) module, which is the strongest component of our architecture. Removing the MSA module resulted in a sharp performance decline, with the ROC-AUC on the Disney dataset dropping from 93.1% to 84.5%, confirming that the integration of localized structural features and global attribute dependencies is critical for identifying subtle anomalous signatures. Overall, these findings validate the effectiveness of GAD-Net in capturing complex graph patterns that traditional reconstruction-based models often overlook.
